🎓 Guided Tour: Understanding Post-Quantum Cryptography

Step 1: Try RSA encryption

Enter a number (like 42) in the RSA box and click "encrypt". Do it twice. Notice how the result is the same both times. This is called deterministic encryption.

Step 2: Try PQC encryption

Now enter the same number in the PQC box and click "encrypt" twice. This time, the results are different each time! PQC adds random "noise" to each encryption.

Step 3: Why does randomness matter?

Quantum computers (like Shor's algorithm) exploit patterns. If encryption is predictable (like RSA), quantum computers can break it. PQC's randomness hides the pattern, making it quantum-resistant.

Step 4: Try the challenge

Scroll down to the "try this" section and click both challenge buttons. See how RSA gives identical results while PQC gives unique ones. That's the future of secure encryption!
